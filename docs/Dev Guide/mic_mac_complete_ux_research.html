Page-by-page UX requirements
Homepage - first impression goals
Above the fold (0–3 sec)
Hero must answer: WHO are you, WHAT do you sell, WHY should I trust you - in 3 seconds. Brand name visible, product image clear, one primary CTA ("Shop Bestsellers"), trust eyebrow ("Lab Certified · Natural · India Made")
Trust bar (3–8 sec)
Immediately below hero. 4 icons - Natural · Cruelty Free · Made for India · Free shipping ₹999+. Forest green background - signals premium, natural. This section reduces bounce for skeptical visitors.
Social proof (scroll 1)
Bestsellers grid with real review counts. "4.8★ (243 reviews)" on each card drives more conversion than any copy. Customers trust other customers - not brand claims.
PDP - the highest-value page in the site
Above the fold on mobile
Product image takes 60% of screen. Price + sale price immediately visible. ATC button reachable with thumb - not hidden below fold. Badge (Bestseller / % off) builds urgency. Reviews score visible before scrolling.
The ingredients section
This is Mic & Mac's biggest differentiator vs generic soap brands. Visual ingredient cards (Donkey Milk · Red Wine · Aloe) with icon + benefit each. Not a wall of text - scannable cards. Priya reads this. Anita skips it. Both need to be served.
Sticky ATC bar
When main ATC scrolls out of view: sticky bar appears at bottom. Contains: thumbnail + name + price + ATC. Eliminates scroll-up friction. Especially critical on mobile where PDP can be very long. Never let the buy button be unreachable.
Cart + checkout flow - reduce friction at maximum intent
Cart drawer vs page
Cart drawer keeps customer in shopping context. They add soap → drawer slides in → see "₹201 away from free shipping" → add another product. This single change increases AOV more than any upsell strategy.
Free shipping progress bar
"Add ₹201 more for free shipping" with animated bar fill is one of the highest ROI features in D2C ecommerce. Avg order value increases by 15–30% when this is implemented. Threshold: ₹999 (aligns with current announcement).
Guest checkout messaging
"No account needed - checkout as guest" must be visually clear. Below checkout button: show UPI + Razorpay + Visa + COD icons. These payment icons serve as micro-trust signals that significantly reduce checkout abandonment for first-time buyers.
Auth pages - make registration feel rewarding, not required
Login / Register UX principle
Never force login to purchase. Show account benefits on the register page: "Track your orders · Save your wishlist · Exclusive member discounts · Reorder in one click." Make account creation a reward, not a gate.
Post-purchase account nudge
After guest checkout: confirmation page shows "Create your account to track this order" with email pre-filled from checkout. Conversion rate for this nudge is 35–40% - far better than pre-purchase forced registration.

Mobile UX requirements - 75% of traffic is mobile
Critical mobile rules for Mic & Mac
Thumb zone design. ATC button, nav items, cart icon - all reachable in the bottom 2/3 of screen. Nothing important top-left on mobile.

Touch targets minimum 44px. Every button, link, form input - minimum 44×44px. Smaller targets cause mis-taps and frustration.

No hover-dependent interactions. Every hover effect must have a touch equivalent. Wishlist on product card: visible on mobile without hover.

Product gallery swipeable. Horizontal swipe on PDP image gallery. Bullet dots show progress. No tap-only thumbnails on mobile.

Sticky ATC always visible. On mobile PDP, the add-to-cart must be reachable at ALL times while reading the description.
Mobile performance targets
< 2.5s
LCP target (Largest Contentful Paint)
< 100ms
INP target (interaction delay)
< 0.1
CLS (layout shift score)
70+
Google PageSpeed mobile score
Achieve with: srcset images, lazy loading below fold, defer non-critical JS, inline critical CSS, use Shopify CDN for all assets.

Accessibility + inclusivity requirements
WCAG 2.1 AA - minimum standard
All images need descriptive alt text (current site has product name only - not descriptive)

Colour contrast ratio minimum 4.5:1 - forest green (#2D4A3E) on cream (#FAF8F4) passes. Gold (#C8A96E) on white fails - never use gold for body text.

Focus states visible on all interactive elements - keyboard navigation must work completely

Form inputs need associated labels - not just placeholder text

Skip to content link at top of every page (already in our layout - keep it)
India-specific inclusivity
Price always in ₹ - never "$" or generic currency symbol

Amounts formatted Indian style - ₹1,00,000 not ₹100,000 for large numbers. Use Intl.NumberFormat('en-IN')

COD (Cash on Delivery) option - still used by 40%+ of Indian online shoppers, especially Tier 2+

WhatsApp contact as primary support - not just email or phone

Page load optimized for 4G mobile - not assuming high-speed WiFi

Font size minimum 16px body - many users have older phones with smaller screens

Content + copywriting UX rules
Voice and tone
Current voice: Corporate, generic, Wikipedia-style

New voice: Warm, knowledgeable, direct. Like a friend who happens to know skincare chemistry.

"Formulated for maximum purity" → "Made with one goal: skin that feels like yours again"

"Donkey milk soap typically contains…" → "What's inside our Donkey Milk Soap - and why each ingredient is there"
Product description structure
1. One-line brand promise specific to this product
2. "Best for" - skin type + concern (oily, dry, pigmentation, sensitive)
3. 3 key benefits - scannable bullets
4. Ingredient story - not a list, a narrative
5. How to use - numbered, simple
6. Lab certification claim + cruelty-free badge

Never use "high-quality" or "premium" - show, don't tell
Microcopy that converts
ATC button: "Add to Cart" (not "Buy Now" - too aggressive for first touch)

Cart empty: "Your cart is empty - but it doesn't have to be" + bestsellers

Checkout: "You're almost there - just 2 steps to go"

After add to cart: "Great choice! Also loved by people who bought this:"

Wishlist: "Saved for later" not "Added to wishlist"

Stock: "Only 5 left" - not "Low stock" (specific = more urgency)
